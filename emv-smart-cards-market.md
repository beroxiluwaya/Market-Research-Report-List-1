<p><p>EMV smart cards are designed for secure payment transactions and come in two primary types: contact and contactless. Contact cards require physical insertion into a card reader, utilizing a chip for secure data exchange. In contrast, contactless cards enable transactions through proximity, utilizing RFID technology, allowing users to tap their card near a reader for quick payments. Both formats enhance transaction security, reduce fraud risks, and improve user convenience, catering to various consumer preferences in the evolving payments landscape.</p></p>
