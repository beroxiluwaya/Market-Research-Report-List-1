<p><p>The dry etching machine for the compound semiconductor market includes two primary types: physical etching and chemical etching. Physical etching utilizes energetic particles, such as ions, to directly bombard and remove material from the semiconductor surface, offering high precision and minimal residue. In contrast, chemical etching involves reactive gases that chemically interact with the material, allowing for more complex patterns and selectivity in etching. Both methods are crucial for fabricating advanced electronic and optoelectronic devices in the semiconductor industry.</p></p>
