<p><p>Laboratory water pumps are crucial for various applications, and they can be categorized into three main types: electric, magnetic-drive, and pneumatic. Electric pumps use electric motors for efficient fluid transfer, suitable for continuous operations. Magnetic-drive pumps employ a magnetic coupling mechanism to eliminate leaks and enable safe handling of corrosive substances. Pneumatic pumps, powered by compressed air, are ideal for transferring liquids in hazardous environments, offering flexibility and safety in handling volatile fluids. Together, they cater to diverse laboratory needs.</p></p>
