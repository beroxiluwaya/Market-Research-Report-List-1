Each type serves different clinical needs; for example, 2-gang manifolds allow for two concurrent infusions, while 5-gang manifolds support more complex treatments by enabling five simultaneous connections. The choice of manifold depends on factors such as the complexity of patient therapies, the number of medications required, and the efficiency desired in infusion management systems.</p></p>
