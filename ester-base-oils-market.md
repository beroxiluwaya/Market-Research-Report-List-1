<p><p>Ester base oils are synthetic lubricants categorized into three main types: monoesters, diesters, and polyol esters. Monoesters consist of a single fatty acid esterified to an alcohol, offering good lubricity and biodegradability. Diesters, formed from two fatty acids, provide enhanced thermal stability and low-temperature performance. Polyol esters, derived from polyols and fatty acids, exhibit superior viscosity index, compatibility, and oxidation resistance, making them ideal for high-performance applications. Each type serves specific needs in automotive, industrial, and specialty lubricants.</p></p>
