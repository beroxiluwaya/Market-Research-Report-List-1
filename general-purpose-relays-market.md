<p><p>The general purpose relays market is primarily segmented into AC coil and DC coil relays. AC coil relays are designed to operate on alternating current, typically used in applications where voltage variations are common. They offer durability and reliability for controlling larger loads. In contrast, DC coil relays operate on direct current, often favored in low-voltage and battery-operated applications, providing faster switching and lower energy consumption. Both types serve critical roles in automation, control systems, and various industrial applications.</p></p>
