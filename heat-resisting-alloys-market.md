<p><p>Heat resisting alloys are categorized into three main types: iron-based superalloys, nickel-based superalloys, and cobalt-based superalloys. </p><p>Iron-based superalloys are known for their strength and oxidation resistance at high temperatures, making them suitable for applications in gas turbines and industrial furnaces. </p><p>Nickel-based superalloys offer superior heat resistance and corrosion resistance, commonly used in aerospace and power generation. </p><p>Cobalt-based superalloys provide excellent wear resistance and stability at elevated temperatures, frequently utilized in medical implants and high-performance components.</p></p>
