<p><p>Vibration energy harvesting systems are categorized into three main types: linear, nonlinear, and rotational systems. Linear systems rely on periodic vibrations to generate energy through components like piezoelectric materials, offering predictable outputs. Nonlinear systems capitalize on varying vibration frequencies for enhanced energy capture, making them more efficient in dynamic environments. Rotational systems convert rotational motion into electrical power, ideal for applications in machinery and vehicles. Each type serves distinct applications, optimizing energy extraction from different vibration sources.</p></p>
