<p><ul><li>First Generation</li><li>Second Generation</li><li>Third Generation</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The antipsychotics market is categorized into three generations based on their development and mechanisms. First-generation antipsychotics, or typical antipsychotics, primarily target dopamine receptors and are effective for positive symptoms of psychosis but can cause significant side effects. Second-generation antipsychotics, or atypical antipsychotics, address both positive and negative symptoms with a broader mechanism, often resulting in fewer side effects. Third-generation antipsychotics are a newer class focused on stabilizing dopamine activity and have improved side effect profiles and efficacy for various mental disorders.</p></p>
